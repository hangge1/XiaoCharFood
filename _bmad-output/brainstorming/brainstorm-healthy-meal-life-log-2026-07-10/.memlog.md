- (technique by coach) started Job to Be Done + Laddering + One Feature Only
- (idea by coach) The product is not pure health management; it is a food-life memory system that reduces meal anxiety and improves future decisions
- (idea by coach) Three source needs: know what I ate, know what to cook, remember where I ate
- (idea by coach) Meal logging must be lighter than calorie apps; otherwise users will churn
- (idea by coach) Cheat meal tracking should be guilt-aware, recording context and recovery rather than moral failure
- (idea by coach) Recipe recommendation should start from household constraints: who cooks, available time, ingredients, taste, budget
- (idea by coach) Restaurant log should preserve experience: price, occasion, dishes, rating, revisit intent, photos, location
- (idea by coach) MVP could be a shared couple food diary with three tabs: Today, Cook, Places
- (idea by coach) The wedge should be couple/household meal planning, not broad healthy lifestyle
- (idea by coach) A good name direction is meal memory, food ledger, or household eating assistant
- (idea by coach) Avoid medicalized positioning unless nutrition data and compliance are strong
- (idea by coach) Daily habit loop: log a meal in 10 seconds, get a weekly food review, receive tomorrow meal suggestions
- (idea by coach) Differentiator from generic note apps is structured recall and recommendation
- (idea by coach) Differentiator from recipe apps is personal history and household preferences
- (idea by coach) Differentiator from restaurant review apps is private memory and decision support
- (decision by coach) Recommended positioning: a private household food-life assistant, with health awareness but not framed as strict health management
- (decision by coach) Recommended MVP: meal diary plus recipe planning first; restaurant log as simple secondary module
- (insight by coach) The product succeeds if it lowers cognitive load and uncertainty, not if it maximizes nutrition precision
